Mexican Street Taco Recipe

Descripton

Mexican street tacos are a vibrant and flavorful street food staple, typically consisting of soft corn tortillas filled with various meats, such as grilled carne asada, tender carnitas, or spicy chorizo. These tacos are often topped with fresh ingredients like chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, along with salsas that range from mild to fiery. Served with a side of radishes or pickled onions, they offer a delightful balance of textures and flavors, making each bite a burst of authentic Mexican culinary tradition. Perfect for a quick snack or a casual meal, street tacos capture the essence of Mexico's rich food culture.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons reduced sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 ½ pounds skirt steak, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 12 mini flour tortillas, warmed
  • ¾ cup diced red onion
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, combine soy sauce, lime juice, 1 tablespoon canola oil, garlic, chili powder, cumin and oregano.
  2. In a gallon size Ziploc bag or large bowl, combine soy sauce mixture and steak; marinate for at least 1 hour up to 4 hours, turning the bag occasionally.
  3. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon canola oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add steak and marinade, and cook, stirring often, until steak has browned and marinade has reduced, about 5-6 minutes, or until desired doneness.
